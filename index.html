Frequently asked questions

Is my bank statement really never uploaded?

Really. The site is static — there is no server to upload to. Parsing happens in a Web Worker on your device using pdf.js. Open DevTools → Network while converting and you'll see zero requests. You can even convert with Wi-Fi turned off.

How do I know the extracted numbers are correct?

Bank2CSV re-computes the running balance across every transaction and compares it to the balances printed on your statement. Each row gets a green check when it reconciles, and the file gets a confidence score. If something doesn't add up, it's flagged in red instead of silently exported.

Do scanned statements work?

Not yet. Bank2CSV needs a text layer in the PDF (statements downloaded from your bank's website always have one). OCR for scanned or photographed statements is on the roadmap.

Which formats can I export?

CSV (opens in anything, imports into QuickBooks) and XLSX (Excel, Numbers, Google Sheets). Pro can also merge many statements into one workbook — one sheet per statement plus a combined, date-sorted sheet. QBO/OFX export is planned.

What does "lifetime" mean for $29?

You pay once and the license is yours permanently, including all future bank profiles and features in this product. It's not a trial and there is no renewal — we can afford that because there are no servers processing your files.

My bank isn't parsed correctly. What do I do?

Email a redacted sample (block out names/account numbers — the layout is what matters) to support@bank2csv.app. Bank profiles are declarative, so new formats usually ship within a few days.
